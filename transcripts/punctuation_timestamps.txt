PUNCTUATION LESSON — CHAPTER TIMESTAMPS

[00:00] Chapter 1: The Big Idea — All Four Are Pauses
"Okay children, so today we are going to look at four punctuation marks..."
Sets up the core framing: four marks, all pauses, different lengths and jobs. Ends with "what kind of pause is this, and what is it doing."

[00:30] Chapter 2: The Comma — Separation
"Now let us just do the comma first..."
Covers the shape of the comma: lists, asides, names being addressed, and linking words like "however." Ends with "Comma equals separation, equals small pause."

[01:25] Chapter 3: The Semi-Colon — Two Jobs
"Now the semi-colon. The semi-colon is bigger than a comma..."
Job 1 (joining two complete sentences without and/but) and Job 2 (separating list items that already contain commas). Ends with "...95 percent of what the SAT will test you on."

[02:50] Chapter 4: The Colon — Introduction
"Now the colon. The colon is a different beast..."
Colon as a drumroll for lists, conclusions, and headings — plus the critical rule that what comes before must be a complete sentence. Ends with "Before a colon, complete sentence. Always."

[04:00] Chapter 5: The Dash — Bonus Information
"Now the dash. The dash is the friendliest of all four..."
Dash as a "by the way" mark, adding extra info or wrapping mid-sentence asides like commas/parentheses. Ends with the Gigi's example.

[04:40] Chapter 6: The Meta-Lesson — Your SAT Blueprint
"Now here is the meta-lesson..."
The two-question framework: (1) is what comes before complete? (2) what's the relationship — separated, joined, introduced, or extended? Ends with the sign-off.
