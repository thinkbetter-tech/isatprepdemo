Now the dash. The dash is the friendliest of all four. The dash is just there to add extra information. To say "by the way." It gives you more about the sentence that came before.

"I can work with everyone in the office, except Sally." Or with a dash, "I can work with everyone in the office, except Sally." See, the dash is just throwing in some extra information, almost like a side comment.

And the dash can also wrap around information in the middle of a sentence, just like commas do, just like parentheses do. "I went to Gigi's, an upscale Italian restaurant, for dinner." Or with dashes, "I went to Gigi's, an upscale Italian restaurant, for dinner." Same thing. The dashes are wrapping a little bit of bonus information.

Now here is the meta-lesson. When you are doing SAT punctuation questions, don't try to remember all the tiny rules. Just remember the shape of each mark. Comma is a small pause that separates. Semi-colon is a bigger pause that joins two complete sentences when there is no "and" or "but." Colon introduces something, but only after a complete sentence. Dash adds bonus information. Four shapes. That is it.

And for every punctuation question on the SAT, just ask yourself two things. One, is the stuff before the punctuation a complete sentence or not. Two, what is the relationship between what comes before and what comes after. Are they being separated, joined, introduced, or extended. That tells you which mark to use.
